## 5. Shadow Self - Feature Page

### Hero
**Discover the Blind Spots Sabotaging Your Success**
Identify cognitive biases and decision-making patterns that might be holding your business back.

### Overview
As a solo founder, you're the sole decision-maker—which means your blind spots become your business's blind spots. Shadow Self uses AI to analyze your decision-making patterns, identify cognitive biases, and provide personalized coaching to help you make better strategic choices.

### How It Works

#### Step 1: Complete the Assessment
Take a 20-minute assessment designed to reveal your decision-making tendencies and cognitive patterns.

#### Step 2: AI Analysis
Our AI analyzes your responses to identify specific biases, blind spots, and decision-making patterns.

#### Step 3: Personalized Report
Receive a detailed report with specific examples of how your biases might impact business decisions.

#### Step 4: Ongoing Coaching
Get regular prompts, reflection exercises, and warnings when making decisions that align with your blind spots.

### Common Blind Spots We Identify
- **Confirmation Bias**: Seeking information that confirms existing beliefs
- **Sunk Cost Fallacy**: Continuing investments because of past commitments
- **Optimism Bias**: Overestimating success probability
- **Availability Heuristic**: Overweighting recent or memorable information
- **Anchoring Bias**: Over-relying on first information received
- **Loss Aversion**: Preferring to avoid losses over acquiring gains

### What You Get
- **Blind Spot Report**: Detailed analysis of your cognitive biases
- **Impact Examples**: Specific scenarios showing how biases affect decisions
- **Coaching Prompts**: Regular exercises to build awareness
- **Decision Warnings**: Alerts when patterns align with blind spots
- **Progress Tracking**: Quarterly reassessments to measure growth
- **Reflection Exercises**: Guided activities to develop self-awareness

### The Science
Shadow Self is built on decades of behavioral economics and cognitive psychology research, including work by:
- Daniel Kahneman (Thinking, Fast and Slow)
- Dan Ariely (Predictably Irrational)
- Richard Thaler (Nudge)
