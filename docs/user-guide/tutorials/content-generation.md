## Content Best Practices

### 1. Define Your Brand Voice

Set up brand voice guidelines for consistent content:

**Brand Voice Elements:**
- Tone (professional, friendly, authoritative, playful)
- Language style (formal, casual, technical, simple)
- Key phrases and terminology
- Words to avoid
- Personality traits

**Example Brand Voice:**
```
Tone: Professional yet approachable
Style: Clear and concise, avoid jargon
Personality: Helpful, knowledgeable, encouraging
Key phrases: "solo founder", "virtual team", "AI-powered"
Avoid: Hype, exaggeration, technical complexity
```

### 2. Provide Context

Better input = better output:

**‚ùå Vague Request:**
"Write a LinkedIn post about productivity"

**‚úÖ Specific Request:**
"Write a LinkedIn post for solo founders about using AI to improve productivity. Include a personal story, 3 actionable tips, and end with a question to drive engagement. Tone: professional but relatable. Length: 150-200 words."

### 3. Use the Viral Hook Generator

Generate attention-grabbing hooks:

**Hook Types:**
- Contrarian statements
- Surprising statistics
- Personal stories
- Bold predictions
- Controversial opinions
- Pattern interrupts

**Example Hooks:**
```
"I wasted 6 months building the wrong product. Here's what I learned..."

"95% of solo founders make this fatal mistake in their first year."

"Unpopular opinion: You don't need a co-founder to build a successful startup."

"I went from $0 to $50k MRR in 90 days. Here's the exact playbook..."
```

### 4. Optimize for Each Platform

**LinkedIn:**
- Professional tone
- Value-driven content
- Personal stories + insights
- 150-300 words optimal
- Use line breaks for readability
- End with engagement question

**Twitter/X:**
- Concise and punchy
- Thread format for longer content
- Use relevant hashtags (2-3 max)
- Include visuals when possible
- Engage with replies

**Instagram:**
- Visual-first approach
- Storytelling in captions
- Use relevant hashtags (10-15)
- Include call-to-action
- Authentic and personal

**Blog Posts:**
- SEO-optimized headlines
- Clear structure with subheadings
- Scannable content (bullets, lists)
- Internal and external links
- Strong introduction and conclusion

### 5. Create Content Series

Build content momentum with series:

**Weekly Series Ideas:**
- "Monday Motivation" - Inspirational content
- "Tip Tuesday" - Actionable advice
- "Wins Wednesday" - Success stories
- "Throwback Thursday" - Lessons learned
- "Feature Friday" - Product highlights

**Monthly Themes:**
- Month 1: Customer success stories
- Month 2: Industry trends and insights
- Month 3: Behind-the-scenes content
- Month 4: Educational content

## Advanced Techniques

### Content Repurposing

Turn one piece of content into multiple formats:

**Example: Blog Post ‚Üí Multiple Assets**
1. Original: 1500-word blog post
2. LinkedIn post: Key insights summary
3. Twitter thread: 10-tweet breakdown
4. Instagram carousel: Visual highlights
5. Email newsletter: Excerpt with link
6. Video script: Talking points
7. Infographic: Data visualization

**How to Repurpose:**
1. Generate original long-form content
2. Ask: "Repurpose this blog post for LinkedIn"
3. Ask: "Create a Twitter thread from this content"
4. Ask: "Extract key points for Instagram carousel"

### Content Calendar Planning

Plan content in advance:

**Monthly Planning:**
1. Define content themes
2. Identify key dates and events
3. Generate content ideas
4. Create content in batches
5. Schedule publication

**Example Request:**
"Create a 30-day content calendar for LinkedIn targeting solo founders. Include mix of educational, inspirational, and promotional content. Focus on AI and productivity topics."

### A/B Testing

Test different approaches:

**What to Test:**
- Headlines and hooks
- Content length
- Tone and style
- Call-to-action
- Posting times

**Process:**
1. Generate multiple variations
2. Publish different versions
3. Track engagement metrics
4. Identify winning patterns
5. Apply learnings to future content

### SEO Optimization

Optimize blog content for search:

**SEO Elements:**
- Target keyword in title
- Keyword in first paragraph
- Subheadings with related keywords
- Internal and external links
- Meta description
- Alt text for images

**Example Request:**
"Write a 1200-word blog post about 'AI tools for solo founders' optimized for SEO. Target keyword: 'AI tools for entrepreneurs'. Include related keywords, internal linking suggestions, and meta description."

**Request:**
"Create a Twitter thread about common mistakes solo founders make when pricing their products. Include 7-10 tweets with actionable advice. Tone: direct and helpful."

**Generated Content:**
```

I see this mistake constantly. Here are 7 pricing errors that are killing your revenue (and how to fix them) üßµ

2/ Mistake #1: Pricing based on costs instead of value

Your costs don't matter to customers. They care about the value you deliver. If you save them $10k/year, charging $1k/year is a steal‚Äîeven if your costs are $100.

Price on value, not costs.

3/ Mistake #2: Being afraid to charge premium prices

"But competitors charge less!" So what? If you deliver more value, charge more. Premium pricing attracts better customers who value your work.

Race to the bottom = race to bankruptcy.

4/ Mistake #3: Not having a clear pricing page

If customers can't find your pricing, they can't buy. Make it prominent, clear, and simple. No "contact us for pricing" unless you're selling enterprise deals.

Transparency builds trust.

5/ Mistake #4: Too many pricing tiers

3 tiers is optimal. More than that creates decision paralysis. 

Good: Basic, Pro, Enterprise
Bad: Starter, Basic, Standard, Pro, Premium, Enterprise, Ultimate

Keep it simple.

6/ Mistake #5: Not anchoring with a high-tier option

Always show a premium tier, even if few buy it. It makes your mid-tier look reasonable by comparison.

$49/mo looks expensive alone.
$49/mo looks like a deal next to $149/mo.

7/ Mistake #6: Discounting too quickly

Every discount trains customers to wait for sales. Offer value instead: extended trials, bonus features, better onboarding.

Protect your pricing integrity.

8/ Mistake #7: Never raising prices

Your product improves. Your costs increase. Your value grows. Your prices should too.

Grandfather existing customers, charge new customers more. Annual 10-15% increases are normal and expected.

9/ Bonus: Not testing pricing

Your first pricing is a guess. Test different price points, tiers, and packaging. Small changes can double revenue.

A/B test everything.
```

## Troubleshooting

### Content Doesn't Match Brand Voice

**Solutions:**
- Update brand voice guidelines in settings
- Provide more specific tone instructions
- Include examples of preferred style
- Regenerate with refined parameters

### Content Is Too Generic

**Solutions:**
- Add more specific context
- Include unique angles or perspectives
- Provide data points or examples
- Specify what makes your approach different

### Content Is Too Long/Short

**Solutions:**
- Specify exact word count
- Use length presets (short, medium, long)
- Edit generated content
- Regenerate with adjusted parameters

### Can't Find Saved Content

**Solutions:**
- Use search function
- Check folder organization
- Filter by date or tags
- Sort by content type
